What You Should Know

Our mission is to prevent the ever encroaching doom that is climate change and global warming. In NYC, we simply aren't doing enough to prevent the tragedy that is our trash crisis. In NYC, we only recycle 18% of our trash at home only going up 7% when we're at work to 25%.

This probably leads you to ask, "But we have more recyclables, why aren't those being recycled?" The answer to that is sorting, since we would have to search through tons and tons and tons of trash, which is unfeasible. As such, we deal with the problem of improperly sorted recyclables, which is still incredibly difficult, so much so that we often times resort to burying our recyclables.

I recycle by placing purchased plastic bottle and glass bottles into bottle shreders. Of course the intention behind this responsible recycling was money. Yes, you get money out of these machines for each bottle, normally it's around 5 cents or so, but moneys still money so I did it anyway. They removed the one I normally use around 2 or 3 years ago but I've found another one somewhat close by. Either way, it was a good deal, where I'd make back a portion of what I payed while contributing to reducing waste. But then again, we've all recently cut down on plastic bottles given that we're all stuck inside.
